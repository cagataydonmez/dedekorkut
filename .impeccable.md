## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are guitarists, hobbyists, and pedal enthusiasts, while the broader audience also includes curious musicians evaluating boutique effects brands. They typically arrive to discover the Dede Korkut Pedal brand, understand what makes each pedal functionally strong and musically useful, and build enough trust to consider a purchase or follow-up inquiry.

The experience should support both first-time discovery and more detail-oriented product evaluation. Product pages should help users quickly grasp practical use cases, tonal character, standout controls, and why the pedals feel credible in real rigs.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel warm, tonal, and distinctive. Its voice should communicate boutique quality, engineering credibility, and musician-first intent without feeling corporate or mass-market.

Emotionally, the interface should evoke trust, calm, and premium craftsmanship. The site should feel like a specialized builder with taste and conviction, not a large multinational catalog brand.

### Aesthetic Direction
The current direction already provides a strong base: dark, technical, product-forward, and atmospheric. Future work should evolve that into a more premium expression while preserving clarity and practical usability.

Reference brands:
- Strymon: premium presentation, clarity, confidence
- Chase Bliss Audio: distinctiveness, boutique personality, design character
- Keeley Electronics: musician-focused product storytelling and trust

Anti-direction:
- Do not resemble multinational, mass-market pedal brands such as Boss or TC Electronic
- Avoid generic ecommerce layouts, overly corporate presentation, or sterile product grids
- Avoid design choices that feel loud, flashy, or trend-chasing at the expense of calm confidence

Theme guidance:
- Support both light and dark modes
- Maintain a premium, boutique feel in either theme
- Favor tonal depth, tactile materials, restrained contrast accents, and product photography that feels intentional rather than promotional

### Design Principles
1. Lead with trust through clarity. Make product purpose, standout functions, and practical value immediately understandable without marketing haze.
2. Preserve boutique warmth. Pair engineering-forward structure with a tone that still feels human, musical, and crafted.
3. Signal premium quality through restraint. Use deliberate spacing, strong typography, careful contrast, and polished details rather than corporate gloss or visual noise.
4. Design for tonal curiosity. Help users imagine sound, workflow, and feel, not just specs; interfaces should support exploration without becoming cluttered.
5. Stay small-brand distinctive. Every page should feel specialized and memorable, never interchangeable with a large consumer electronics company.

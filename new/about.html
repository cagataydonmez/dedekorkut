About the Brand

Engineered with ears, tuned like a boutique build sheet.

Dede Korkut Pedal began in 2014 with handmade analog builds for local musicians, then moved into DSP without losing the instinct for clear controls, usable tone, and player-first decisions.

The brand sits between workshop pragmatism and musical taste: electronics engineering on one side, the feel of a real pedalboard on the other.

Turkey-based boutique builder. Small-batch feel. Practical musical intent.

Chapter One

Handmade analog pedals for local players.

The first chapter was close to the scene around it: small runs, direct player feedback, and a builder’s instinct for what feels understandable on the floor instead of only interesting on paper.

Chapter Two

The technology changed. The instinct did not.

Moving into DSP was not a reset. It was a way to expand delay, reverb, and modulation possibilities without abandoning the calm control language that made the early pedals feel approachable.

  • Player-first control layouts stayed central.
  • Functions expanded without hiding behind screens.
  • Workflow stayed more important than feature count.

Chapter Three

Today’s pedals are engineered for rigs, not showrooms.

Current designs are built to earn trust in real use: strong core sounds, immediate controls, and enough tonal depth to stay interesting long after first contact.
